Experiences from Silicon Valley Days

After spending 7 years with General Electric, 2 1/2 of which were on their Technical Marketing Training Program, I was recruited by a former GE executive that had left to become CEO of the first Wi-Ficompany, Proxim, in Silicon Valley. I moved there right after the 89' earthquake The internet was new, Cisco Systems had just been founded by a husband and wife team working for Stanford University, Steve Jobs had left Apple to start NEXT & Pixar. How the world has changed. Following are a few of the startups and turnarounds that I participated in. 

Proxim - Mission Impossible

Proxim had been producing radio modules for warehouse inventory management companies, and providing turn-key tracking solutions for large military projects when the FCC - Federal Communications Commission opened up Spectrum for unlicensed use enabling a new form of communications between computer systems.  Clunky laptops and tablets were just starting to be developed, and the internet was a few years off.  Google would be launched 9 years later. 

Unisys Network Enable

Unisys was making most of their revenue from mainframe maintenance contracts when they decided to create some divisions in early stage technologies like multivendor network systems integration - Network Enable.  I joined as San Francisco District Manager and was quickly promoted to Director of the Western region.  We integrated products from hundreds of vendors receiving information and placing orders over fax machines. I came with the idea of converting all of our internal processes, vendor information, data sheets, and pricing to Lotus notes and sold the idea to management.  It totally transformed the way we did business.

Communications Intelligence Corp

CIC, funded by Stanford Research, was a perfect example of spending millions of dollars developing complex products without doing the proper market research. The created handwriting recognition products for the Japanese & Chinese markets, each having roughly 10k unique character symbols, and no revenue generation.  One of the engineers created a simple product where you could write single characters on a screen. I sold the heck out of it to Microsoft, Ericsson / Symbian (I was the first to sell to them), and anyone else with touch based device. We had some strong biometric signature verification tech and integrated it into Adobe, creating the first digital signature solution. I put a team together to license this to systems integrators and large enterprises, We won a major deal with Charles Schwab for enrolling new accounts.  Two yeas later, when Palm went public, we were the third highest volume on NASDAQ for a week, and reached a multi-billion dollar market cap.

Aeroscout

I went on to work for AeroScout was a very early stage Israeli startup funded by Intel and worked as VP Business Development where we had to find & create markets where none existed before with technology that was changing by the day. It was later sold to Stanely Healthcare.

AirDefense

AirDefense, a wireless intrusion detection & protection system was founded by an ex Unisys collegue and hired my as VP of the Western Region. It was collogue to Motorola.
